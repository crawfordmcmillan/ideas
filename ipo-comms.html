Your IPO has a narrative problem you don't know about yet

Right now, somewhere in the market, investors are forming opinions about your company. Analysts are building mental models. Journalists are deciding if you're a story worth covering.

This is happening whether you're ready or not.

The window between "we're thinking about going public" and "we just filed" is when your story gets locked. Not the roadshow. Not the pricing call. The months before anyone knows you're coming.

Most companies spend this time on lawyers and bankers. They assume the story will come together later.

It doesn't.

By the time they're on the roadshow, the narrative is already baked. The analyst consensus is already forming. The first-day pop (or drop) is already in motion.

The quiet period isn't when your story starts. It's when it gets locked.

Companies that nail the pre-filing window don't just get better coverage. They get better valuations.

The narrative precedes the numbers. The story shapes the multiple. Our most recent client achieved a valuation that surprised even their bankers. Not because the numbers changed. Because the story was already in place when the numbers showed up.

Why large agencies fail at this

Media team files the press releases. Digital team runs the LinkedIn. IR team preps the roadshow deck. Executive coach works with the CEO.

By the time your story reaches the market, it's four different stories. The S-1 says one thing. The CEO profile says another. The digital footprint contradicts both.

Analysts notice. Investors notice. Your valuation reflects the confusion.

We run all four as one campaign because that's how the market receives you: as one company with one story.

The narrative has to be consistent before it can be compelling.
